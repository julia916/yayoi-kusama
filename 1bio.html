Biography

Yayoi Kusama is an avant-garde sculptor, painter, and novelist, born in Nagano Prefecture.
Kusama studied traditional Japanese painting and traveled to the United States in 1957.
She exhibited large paintings, soft sculptures, and environmental sculptures using mirrors and electric lights.

In the late 1960s, Kusama staged many happenings such as body painting festivals,
fashion shows and anti-war demonstrations. Launched media-related activities such as film production and
newspaper publication. In 1968, the film “Kusama's Self-Obliteration" which Kusama produced and starred in.
She won a prize at the Fourth International Experimental Film Competition in Belgium and the Second Maryland Film
Festival and the second prize at the Ann Arbor Film Festival. Held exhibitions and staged happenings also in
various countries in Europe.
